   Shankar and Shah (2003) and Lessmann and Seidel (2017) showed that Brazil is one of the most

The present work aims to analyze the regional disparity of income in Brazil in the last forty years, using

the wage structure effect is more important than the composition effect to explain the regional income
inequality. Finally, it was observed that schooling is more important since 1991 and in the higher
quantiles, while the agricultural sector is more important before 1991 and in the lowest quantiles.
