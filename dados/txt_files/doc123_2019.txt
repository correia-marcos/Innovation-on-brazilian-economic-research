                                             Abstract

      Public policy evaluation is in many cases supported by its assumption of perfect
      competition, which is not always true, and can often influence the outcome of the
      analysis. In other cases, public policy itself is capable of affecting the degree of mar-
      ket imperfection. The objective of this study is to evaluate the effect of the policy of
      exemption on the degree of imperfection in the market of goods (final product) and
      labor market, but also to analyze the degree of competitiveness of both markets.
      For this, data from the Annual Industrial Survey were used from 2007 to 2015. The
      results indicate that the policy stimulated the competitiveness of the final goods
      market, which is characterized by imperfect competition, and had a positive effect
      on the power of workers‚Äô bargain in the labor market, where perfect competition
      prevails.
