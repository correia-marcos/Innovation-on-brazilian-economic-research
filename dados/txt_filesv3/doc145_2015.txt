Abstract

The study evaluates the Constitutional Financing Fund for the Center-West (FCO) in the state
of Goi´as between the years 2004 and 2011, using RAIS microdata, and loans from the FCO
“Empresarial” Program. The results show a positive and statistically signiﬁcant impact of the
FCO on employment and wage growth at the ﬁrm level. We use the Propensity Score Matching
(PSM) and the Generalized Propensity Score (GPS) methods. The GPS identiﬁes the treatment
variable as continuous. This approach is original in the literature on regional funds evaluation.
Moreover, the dose response results indicate that the loans values aﬀect wages and employment,
as well the PSG results suggest that around the limit of R$ 200,000.00 the proportional variation
of employment and wages are higher as higher are the loans.
