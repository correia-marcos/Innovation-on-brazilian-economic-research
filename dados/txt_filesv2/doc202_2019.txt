ABSTRACT 
 
 
In 2001, the Brazilian Federal Government, concerned about the housing deficit created 
the  "Residential  Lease  Program"  (PAR).  However,  coupled  with  the  construction  of 
quality housing and reducing the housing deficit, there was also an improvement in the 
basic sanitation structure of the population that was contemplated by the program. Faced 
with  this  fact,  the  present  study  analyzes  the  effect  of  RAP  on  deaths  due  to  sanitary 
diseases.  In  order  to  reach  our  objective,  we  use  the  Regression  Discontinuity,  which 
guarantees  us  robust  estimators  of  endogeneity.  In  addition,  our  empirical  strategy  is 
strengthened  by  robustness  testing  and  heterogeneous  responses.  Thus,  as  a  result,  we 
found that PAR can reduce deaths caused by sanitary diseases, and this effect overflows 
over time. 
 
Keywords: Housing; Programa de Arrendamento Residencial; Housing Financing;
