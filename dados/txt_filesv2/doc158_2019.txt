Abstract:  This paper presents evidence of the existence of agglomeration economies 
generated by spatially concentrated industries. The object of analysis is the textile and 
clothing industry of the state of Pernambuco and seeks to answer: if the economies of 
agglomeration  act  as  a  force  to  attract  new  establishments  of  the  textile  and  clothing

new  firms.  The  empirical  evidences  of  this  article  prove  that  the  effects  of  the 
agglomeration economies decrease with the distance, indicating for the importance of the 
geography to the economic sciences. The results are validated by robustness tests and 
point  to  the  existence  of  economies  of  urbanization  and  location,  the  latter  having  a 
greater effect on the expected number of new establishments in the industry.
