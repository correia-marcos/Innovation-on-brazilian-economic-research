ABSTRACT 

China has established its leading role on the world economy, while Latin American countries do not seem 
to  have  strengthened  their  role  as  exporters  of  industrialized  products.  Chinese  economic  growth  is  a 
challenge  for  the  Latin  American  countries,  especially  because  of  the  importance  of  the  exports  of 
industrialized products. Therefore, the aim of this paper is to determine the impact of China's performance 
in world trade on the exports of products with technological content from Brazil and Mexico in the period 
from 2001 to 2016. This paper focuses on the effect on products classified as labor-intensive, economies of 
scale,  specialized  suppliers  and  intensive  in  research  and  development,  according  to  the  Pavitt  (1984) 
classification. In terms of statistical treatment, a two-stage dynamic panel data model is applied. Our results 
indicate that China‚Äôs exports displace exports from Brazil and Mexico only when the Asian country enters 
the partner markets of Latin American countries. In addition, the results indicate a possible loss of market 
share of Brazil and Mexico on their partners over time.
